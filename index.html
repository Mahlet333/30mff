About the Film

I thought I came to study (but the campus studied me) is about a student who slowly begins to realize something unsettling. University is not just about classes or deadlines. It is about the spaces you move through every day. At first, those spaces seem ordinary: a dorm room, a humming laundry machine, a flickering hallway light, the soft glow of a laptop screen at three in the morning. But over time, the student senses something else. The campus is not just a backdrop. It is paying attention. It watches. It regulates. It does not ask anything directly, but somehow, you begin to change. Your thoughts. Your rhythms. Even your silences.

The film drifts quietly through these places. There are no fast cuts. No loud sounds. It invites you to slow down and feel. To notice. To ask: what is really shaping me here? This is not a scary story. There are no monsters. No villains. Just architecture. Routine. Quiet pressure.

Shot entirely on location at NYU Abu Dhabi, using real campus spaces, the film tries to capture what it actually feels like to live on campus. Being busy but still stuck. Being watched, but not by cameras. Just by the space itself. The building. The air. The system. The film is short. Only ninety seconds. But it might stay with you longer.

What space studied you?

Analysis

The Empty Hallway

The Surface: A long, sterile hallway stretching toward nowhere, with fluorescent lights that flicker just slightly too often.

What the Campus Was Doing: The hallway is designed to create the illusion of being observed. Notice how the light creates shadows that follow you from multiple angles.

Missed Detail: There\'s a shadow that appears for three frames with no visible source.

Reflection: How often do you feel watched when nobody is there?

The Library at 3AM

The Surface: Empty chairs arranged in perfect symmetry. Books no one is reading. The sound of pages turning.

What the Campus Was Doing: Time behaves differently here. The clocks run at 0.98x normal speed—just slow enough to be imperceptible but enough to keep you longer than you planned.

Missed Detail: The same student appears in the background three times, wearing three slightly different outfits.

Reflection: When was the last time you checked a clock twice and felt something was wrong?

The Dining Hall

The Surface: Hundreds of conversations happening at once, blending into white noise. Food being consumed mechanically.

What the Campus Was Doing: The acoustics are designed to amplify certain frequencies—making private conversations public while administrative announcements fade into the background.

Missed Detail: Every tray contains the exact same arrangement of food, down to the placement of silverware.

Reflection: How much of your daily routine is your choice versus what the space decided for you?

The Laundry Room

The Surface: Washing machines spinning in hypnotic rhythm. The sound of water being forced through fabric.

What the Campus Was Doing: This is one of the few places where students can be alone with their thoughts, so the institution ensures it\'s as uncomfortable as possible. Notice the harsh lighting, the metallic surfaces that amplify sound.

Missed Detail: One washing machine never stops, even when unplugged.

Reflection: What part of you has never been given permission to just be?'

Filmmaker

About the Filmmaker

Mahlet Atresaw Andarge is a filmmaker and student at NYU Abu Dhabi. Her work explores the subtle ways environments shape our inner lives, with a focus on memory, routine, and the architecture of feeling. She believes the spaces we inhabit are never neutral—they watch, they remember, and they change us.
